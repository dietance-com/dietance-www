Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a dietitian and a nutritionist?

A dietitian is a certified and licensed health professional with a degree in dietetics, and they complete extensive clinical training. This allows them to provide medical nutrition therapy for various health conditions. A nutritionist, while knowledgeable about food and nutrition, may not have the same level of education, training, or licensing to treat medical conditions.

What should I expect during my first consultation?

During your first visit, your dietitian will conduct a comprehensive assessment of your health history, current eating habits, and lifestyle. This helps them understand your unique needs and goals. Based on this, they'll create a personalised nutrition plan tailored just for you. You'll have plenty of time to ask questions and discuss any concerns you may have.

Will I have to give up all my favorite foods?

No, not at all! Our goal is to help you build a healthy relationship with food, not to restrict you. We believe in moderation and balance. We'll work with you to incorporate your favorite foods into your plan while making sustainable changes that fit your lifestyle. It's about finding a balance that works for you.

How long will it take to see results?

The timeline for results varies for everyone, as it depends on your specific goals and commitment to the plan. Some people notice improvements in energy and well-being within a few weeks, while significant changes may take longer. We'll monitor your progress and make adjustments to the plan to ensure you're on the right track for long-term success.

Do you offer virtual consultations?

Yes, we do! We offer flexible online consultations to make it convenient for you to receive guidance from the comfort of your own home. Our virtual sessions are just as personalised and effective as in-person appointments.
